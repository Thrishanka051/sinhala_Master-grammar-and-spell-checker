1. Ensure proper subject-verb-object (SVO) structure.
   Example: "මම පොතක් කියවමි." is correct. Avoid: "මම කියවමි පොතක්."

2. Avoid redundant particles such as "ක්", "ම", and "ක්වෙ." Use minimal particles.
   Example: "පොතක් කියවමි." is preferred over "පොතක් කියවමිනි."

3. Use correct verb conjugation depending on tense.
   Example: Present: "කැවිමි" Past: "කෑවෙමි" Future: "කනවා."

4. Keep sentence length concise. Break long sentences into simpler parts.
   Example: "මම පොත් දෙකක් කියවා පාඩම් ලිවිය." is preferred over "මම පොත් දෙකක් කියවා සිටිමි එසේ මට පාඩම් ලිවිය."

5. Proper spacing between words is critical.
   Example: Avoid: "මමපොතක්." Correct: "මම පොතක්."

6. Use the correct plural forms for nouns and adjectives.
Example: Singular: "පොතක්" → Plural: "පොත්."
Avoid incorrect forms like: "පොතලා."

7. Ensure agreement between subject and verb in gender and number.
Example: Masculine: "ඔහු කියවයි." Feminine: "ඇය කියවයි."
Incorrect: "ඔහු කියවයි ඇය."

8. Use appropriate honorifics based on context and social norms.
Example: Informal: "ඔබ කුමක් කරයිද?" → Formal: "ඔබ කුමක් කරන්නේද?"
Avoid mixing informal and formal styles in the same sentence.

9. Avoid mixing spoken and written forms in the same sentence.
Example: Spoken: "මට බඩගිනියි." Written: "මට ආහාර අභිලාෂය පවතී."
Do not combine: "මට ආහාර බඩගිනියි."

10. Use the correct cases for nouns (nominative, accusative, etc.) based on context.
Example: Subject: "මම පොතක් කියවමි." Object: "මට පොතක්."
Avoid: "මට කියවමි පොතක්."

11. Avoid overuse of emphasis particles like "ම," "ද," and "එ."
Example: "මම ගමට ගියෙමි" is preferred over "මමම ගමටම ගියෙමිනි."

12. Use proper punctuation to clarify meaning.
Example: Add commas or full stops where appropriate:
Correct: "මම, ගමට ගියෙමි."
Avoid: "මම ගමට ගියෙමි ම."

13. Write numbers in full for formal texts, and use numerals for informal texts.
Example: Formal: "දහය." Informal: "10."
Avoid mixing: "දහය 10."

14. Use correct syntax for interrogative sentences.
Example: Question: "ඔයා කුමක් කරන්නේද?" Avoid: "ඔයා කරන්නේ කුමක්?"

15. Avoid literal translations from other languages.
Example: Correct Sinhala structure: "ඔයා කාලෙකුට පසුව එනවාද?"
Avoid: "ඔයා කල් පසුව එනවාද?" (literal translation of "Are you coming after some time?").

16. Avoid unnecessary repetition of words or ideas.
Example: "මම ගමට ගියෙමි." is preferred over "මම ගමට ගියෙමි ගමට."

17. Correct use of pronouns based on subject, object, or possessive case.
Example: Subject: "ඔහු." Object: "ඔහුව." Possessive: "ඔහුගේ."
Avoid: "ඔහු ඔහුව ඔහුගේ."

18. Use correct conjunctions to link phrases and clauses.
Example: "මම කියවමි සහ ලියමි."
Avoid: "මම කියවමි ලියමි."

19. Ensure appropriate word order for emphasis when needed.
Example: Regular: "මම පොතක් කියවමි." Emphatic: "මම කියවමි පොතක්ම."
Avoid unnecessary emphatic order: "කියවමි මම පොතක්ම."

20. Avoid using colloquial terms in formal writing unless specified.
Example: Formal: "ආහාර ගනු ලැබේ." Colloquial: "කෑම ගන්නවා."
Use consistent tone throughout.

21. Use proper noun declensions based on their grammatical role.  
   Example: Subject: "මම." Object: "මට."  
22. Avoid unnecessary honorifics with close relationships.  
   Example: Use "ඔයා" instead of "ඔබ" when addressing a friend.  
23. Use appropriate reflexive pronouns for clarity.  
   Example: "ඔහුම" (himself), "ඇයම" (herself).  
24. Ensure possessive pronouns agree with the noun.  
   Example: "මගේ පොතක්," not "මගේ පොත්."  
25. Use "ඔවුන්" for plural humans and "එවා" for non-human objects.  
   Example: Correct: "ඔවුන් පැමිණියහ."  
26. Avoid overuse of demonstrative pronouns like "මේ" and "ඒ."  
27. Place pronouns correctly relative to verbs.  
   Example: "ඔහු කියවයි පොතක්."  
28. Use "අපි" for inclusive groups and "අප" for exclusive groups.  
29. Avoid incorrect pluralization of pronouns.  
   Example: Incorrect: "ඔබලාගේ." Correct: "ඔවුන්ගේ."  
30. Use gender-neutral pronouns only when gender is irrelevant.  

31. Use consistent tense throughout a sentence.  
   Example: Avoid: "ඔහු කියවයි, පසුව කෑවා."  
32. Ensure verbs agree with subjects in number and gender.  
33. Use appropriate auxiliary verbs when needed.  
   Example: "මම ලිව්වේය."  
34. Avoid combining multiple tenses in a single verb phrase.  
   Example: "කෙරුණාය" is incorrect. Use "කළේය."  
35. Use active or passive voice based on clarity.  
   Example: Active: "මම කෑවා." Passive: "ආහාරය කෑවේ මම."  
36. Use "කළයුතුය" for necessity and "කියවිය යුතුය" for obligation.  
37. Conjugate verbs properly for conditional sentences.  
   Example: "ඔයා එනවා නම්, මම යනවා."  
38. Use causative verbs correctly.  
   Example: "කරවයි" (makes do), not "කරයි."  
39. Avoid redundancy in conjugated verbs.  
   Example: "කියවමින් කියවීම" is incorrect.  
40. Use imperatives for commands, avoiding unnecessary politeness.  
   Example: "එන්න," not "ආයුබෝවන් එන්න."  

41. Place the main verb at the end of the sentence.  
42. Use subordinate clauses for complex ideas.  
   Example: "මම දන්නා විදියට, ඔහු එනවා."  
43. Avoid splitting subject and predicate unnecessarily.  
   Example: Incorrect: "මම පොතක් කියවමින්, එසේ ලිව්වා."  
44. Use prepositions correctly based on the case of nouns.  
   Example: "සඳහා" with dative: "මට."  
45. Avoid run-on sentences. Break them into smaller, logical parts.  
46. Maintain parallel structure when listing actions.  
   Example: "ඔහු කියවයි, ලියයි, අඳින්නයි."  
47. Use expletives ("ඇත්තෙන්ම") sparingly to emphasize.  
48. Avoid unnecessary inversions for poetic effect in prose.  
49. Use interjections appropriately for emotion or emphasis.  
   Example: "අයියේ! මට උදව් කරන්න."  
50. Place adverbs close to the verb they modify.  
  
51. Use synonyms for variety but ensure they match the tone.  
52. Avoid archaic words in modern writing unless for style.  
53. Ensure correct use of homonyms based on context.  
   Example: "තෙර" (shore) vs. "තේර" (choose).  
54. Avoid mixing Pali or Sanskrit terms unnecessarily.  
55. Use regional dialects only when contextually appropriate.  
56. Avoid using loanwords unless unavoidable.  
57. Match adjectives to the tone of the subject.  
   Example: "දකුණු" for formal, "බටහිර" for informal.  
58. Avoid overloading sentences with multiple adjectives.  
59. Use "එක්" for singular items and "වෙත" for directions.  
60. Avoid literal translations from foreign idioms.  
61. Keep sentence openings diverse. Avoid starting with "මම" repeatedly.  
62. Use rhetorical questions sparingly.

64. Avoid abrupt transitions without connecting phrases.
